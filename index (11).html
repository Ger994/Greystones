The house

A century old whinstone house, quietly brought into the present.

Behind the stone walls and leaded glass sits an interior that has been restored from end to end. The panelled hall and stained glass belong to the house's first century; the marble kitchen island, cinema room and deep sofas belong firmly to this one. It photographs like a heritage property and lives like a five star hotel.

The house stands in mature private gardens with open views across the Renfrewshire countryside to the water beyond. Sixteen guests fit without it ever feeling crowded, and a party of twelve has room to spread out, cook, screen a film and lose an afternoon at the pool table.

Inside

Room by room

Every space at Greystones earns its keep, from the grand hall that greets your guests to the attic games room that keeps them up too late.

First impressions

The Great Hall

Original timber panelling, stained-glass roundels and a slate floor set the tone the moment the front door opens. This is where the champagne gets poured, the coats come off and the weekend properly begins.

The heart of the house

The Marble Kitchen

A statement marble island anchors a fully fitted modern kitchen, with glazed doors opening straight onto the terrace and lawn. There is space for a crowd to cook together, or to stand back with a glass while a chef takes over.

Gathering

Drawing Room & Conservatory

Deep sofas around the fire flow into a glass roofed conservatory with views the length of the garden and far beyond it. Morning coffee happens at one end of the day here, nightcaps at the other.

Rest

The Bedrooms

Beautifully dressed bedrooms sleep twelve to sixteen across the house, from the principal suite with its bay-window sitting area and long views over the water, to generous rooms for family and friends.

Play

Cinema & Games Room

Under the eaves: a full cinema setup with tiered seating, a pool table, and table tennis. Rainy day insurance, a teenage sanctuary, and the natural home of the late night rematch.

Celebrations

Weddings at Greystones

Exclusive use of the whole house and grounds means the wedding weekend runs your way. Ceremony on the lawn, dinner in a marquee, dancing until the small hours, and everyone you love asleep under one roof.

The ceremony

Exchange vows on the lawn against open countryside, in the conservatory, or before the fire in the Great Hall. Three settings, one address.

The wedding breakfast

A marquee on the main lawn seats your full guest list; smaller parties dine in the house itself. Our private catering team builds the menu around you.

The morning after

No taxis at midnight. The wedding party wakes up at Greystones to a long breakfast, a walk round the gardens and the retelling of the night before.

The main lawn is level, private and framed by mature trees. A natural stage for a marquee, a ceremony arch, or a hundred chairs facing the view.

Good to know

Questions, answered

Is Greystones exclusively ours for the stay?

Yes. Every booking is exclusive use. The whole house, gardens and grounds are yours for the duration of your stay.

Can we hold our wedding ceremony at the house?

Yes. Couples hold ceremonies on the lawn, in the conservatory or in the Great Hall, and we'll help you coordinate celebrants, licensing and suppliers when you enquire.

How does catering work?

Choose full private-chef service, event catering for larger wedding numbers, or self-catering in the main kitchen. A separate professional catering kitchen supports chefs and caterers throughout your stay.

Can day guests join us beyond the 16 sleeping?

Yes. For weddings and events, additional day guests can be accommodated, typically with a marquee on the main lawn. Tell us your numbers and we'll quote accordingly.

Is there parking?

Yes, the private drive comfortably takes the whole party's vehicles.
